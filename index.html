About the Soft Robotics Lab

The Soft Robotics Lab at IIT Delhi, housed within the Department of Applied Mechanics, is at the forefront of research and innovation in soft robotics, exosuits, and flexible electronics. Our mission is to develop next-generation robotic systems that are safe, adaptive, and seamlessly integrate with humans and their environment.

What is Soft Robotics?

Soft robotics is a cutting-edge field that focuses on designing robots using highly compliant materials, mimicking the flexibility and adaptability found in living organisms. Unlike traditional rigid robots, soft robots can:
  • Safely interact with humans and delicate objects
  • Navigate through unstructured or dynamic environments
  • Adapt their shape and function to specific tasks
Applications include medical devices, wearable technologies, search and rescue operations, and advanced manufacturing.

Exosuits: Enhancing Human Capability

Exosuits are wearable robotic devices designed to assist and augment human movement. In our lab, we focus on developing lightweight, flexible exosuits that:
  • Support rehabilitation for patients with mobility impairments
  • Reduce fatigue and risk of injury for workers in physically demanding jobs
  • Enhance strength and endurance for soldiers and first responders
Our exosuits leverage soft actuators and sensors to provide intuitive, responsive assistance while maintaining wearer comfort and mobility.

Flexible Electronics: The Backbone of Soft Robotics

Flexible electronics are integral to the advancement of soft robotics. These stretchable, bendable circuits and sensors enable:
  • Real-time monitoring of human motion and physiological signals
  • Seamless integration with soft robotic structures
  • Enhanced safety and functionality in wearable devices
Our research explores new materials and fabrication techniques to create robust, high-performance flexible electronic systems for robotics and healthcare.

Research Highlights

  • Development of soft robotic grippers for delicate object manipulation
  • Design of bio-inspired actuators and sensors
  • Creation of wearable exosuits for gait assistance and rehabilitation
  • Integration of flexible electronics for smart sensing and control
